DFS, BFS & Khans Algorithm


Depth-First Search (DFS) and Breadth-First Search (BFS) are fundamental graph traversal algorithms used in computer science to explore and analyze graphs. DFS systematically explores as far as possible along each branch before backtracking, making it useful for tasks such as topological sorting, detecting cycles, and finding connected components. In contrast, BFS explores neighbors of a node before moving on to its children, making it suitable for shortest path problems and level-order traversal. Khan's Algorithm, on the other hand, is primarily employed for topological sorting of directed acyclic graphs (DAGs). It works by iteratively removing nodes with zero in-degree, gradually revealing a linear ordering of the vertices. These algorithms represent essential tools for solving a variety of graph-related problems efficiently.
